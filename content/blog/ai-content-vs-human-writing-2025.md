However, Google’s "E-E-A-T" (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness) model is specifically designed to filter out "flavourless" AI spam.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Google penalize AI content?
No. Google penalizes low-quality content that is produced primarily to manipulate search rankings. If your AI-assisted content is helpful to users, it can rank #1.

### How can I tell if content is written by AI?
There are "AI detectors," but they are notoriously unreliable in 2025. The best way to tell is by the lack of original thought, repetitive sentence structures, and an absence of a clear "personality."

### Should I use AI for my blog?
Yes, but as an assistant, not a replacement. Use it to help you brainstorm and outline, but ensure a human expert provides the actual insights and final edit.

### What is E-E-A-T?
It stands for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. These are the four pillars Google uses to evaluate the quality of content and the reliability of its creator.
