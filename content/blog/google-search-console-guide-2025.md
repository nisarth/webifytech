## Setting Up for Success: Verification and Configuration

Before you can access the data, you must verify ownership of your site. In 2025, we recommend Domain-level verification via DNS, as it captures all subdomains and protocol variations (HTTP vs. HTTPS).

### 1. Submitting Your Sitemap
The first task in any **Google Search Console guide** is ensuring Google knows where your pages are. Submit your `sitemap.xml` (which we provide for all our [LINK: services]) to ensure rapid crawling and indexing.

### 2. Monitoring the Indexing Report
This is where you'll find "Crawl Errors." If Google finds a page but decides not to index it, you need to know why. Common issues include "Crawled - currently not indexed" or "Excluded by 'noindex' tag."

## Analyzing the Performance Report

The Performance report is where you see the "magic." You can track:
- **Queries**: The exact keywords people are typing into Google to find you.
- **Pages**: Which of your articles (like those in our [LINK: resources] section) are driving the most traffic.
- **Countries**: Where your audience is located.
- **Devices**: How many people are finding you on mobile vs. desktop.

## Fixing Technical Issues with GSC

GSC is also your primary tool for technical health:
- **Core Web Vitals**: Monitor your LCP, FID, and CLS scores across your entire site.
- **Mobile Usability**: Google will alert you if any elements are too close together or if text is too small for mobile users.
- **Security & Manual Actions**: This is where Google notifies you of malware or if you've been penalized for violating their guidelines.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is Google Search Console used for?
Google Search Console is a free service provided by Google that helps you monitor, maintain, and troubleshoot your site's presence in Google Search results. It helps you understand how Google sees your site and optimize its performance.

### How do I see which keywords I rank for?
Go to the 'Performance' report. Under the 'Queries' tab, you'll see a list of keywords that have earned you impressions and clicks. You can filter this data by date, page, and country to see your most valuable organic triggers.

### How do I fix indexing issues in GSC?
First, identify the problem in the 'Indexing' report. If it's a 404 error, set up a 301 redirect. If it's a 'noindex' issue, check your page's meta tags. Once fixed, you can use the 'Validate Fix' button to ask Google to recrawl the page.

### What is the difference between Google Analytics and Search Console?
Google Analytics tracks what users do *on* your site (sessions, bounce rate, conversion). Google Search Console tracks how users find you *in Google Search* (queries, impressions, CTR). Both are essential for a complete digital strategy during the [LINK: about] phase of growth.

### How often should I check Google Search Console?
For most businesses, checking GSC once a week is a good habit. However, you should check it immediately after any major site update or if you notice a sudden drop in organic traffic.
