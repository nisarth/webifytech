## Technical Foundations for International Success

Before you write a single word in a new language, your site's structure must be ready.

### 1. Choosing Your URL Structure
- **ccTLDs (e.g., .de, .fr)**: Best for local trust and high rankings in specific countries.
- **Subdirectories (e.g., example.com/fr)**: Best for concentrating domain authority and easier management.
- **Subdomains (e.g., fr.example.com)**: A middle-ground often used for complex site architectures.

### 2. Mastering Hreflang Tags
Hreflang tags are the most critical technical element of international SEO. They tell Google which version of a page to show to which user based on their language and location. Mismanaging these is the #1 reason for "duplicate content" issues in multi-lingual sites.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are hreflang tags?
They are HTML attributes that tell search engines the language and geographical targeting of a webpage. They ensure users land on the version of your site that is most relevant to them.

### Should I use a different domain for every country?
If you have the budget to manage multiple sites and want the maximum local trust, yes. However, for 90% of businesses, using subdirectories on a single .com domain is the more efficient choice.

### Does AI translation help with international SEO?
It's a great starting point, but in 2025, human oversight is still required to ensure the "tone" and "cultural relevance" are correct. Google prioritizes content that feels authentically local.

### How do I track international search performance?
Use Google Search Console's "International Targeting" report (though this is evolving in 2025) and filter your performance data by country to see where you are winning or losing.

### What is the biggest mistake in international SEO?
Treating every country in a language group (like Spanish or English) the same. A user in Mexico has different needs and search behaviors than a user in Spain. This nuance is a core focus in our [LINK: about] discovery phase.
