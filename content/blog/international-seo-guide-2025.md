## Strategy 1: The Technical Architecture of Global SEO
Before you translate a single word, you must choose the right URL structure.
- **ccTLDs (Country Code Top-Level Domains)**: `website.co.uk`, `website.fr`. Best for local authority, but most expensive to maintain.
- **Subdirectories**: `website.com/uk/`, `website.com/fr/`. Our preferred method for most brands because it consolidates your domain authority. (Verified during our [LINK: technical-seo-audit-2025]).
- **Subdomains**: `uk.website.com`, `fr.website.com`. Easier to set up on different servers but can be harder for Google to see as a single entity.

## Strategy 2: Hreflang Mastery: The Language of Google
Hreflang is a small bit of code that tells Google: "This page is the French version for users in France."

## FAQ

### How long does International SEO take to work?
Because you are often starting from "zero" authority in new markets, it can take 3-6 months to see significant organic traffic. However, if your main domain is already authoritative, new subdirectories can often start ranking within 4-8 weeks.

### Is "Auto-Translation" (like G-Translate) good for SEO?
No. While it has improved, it still creates "thin" content that lacks the nuance required for high rankings. In 2025, [LINK: ai-content-vs-human-writing-2025] a hybrid approach—AI for the base, human for the localization—is the winning formula.

### What is the most common international SEO mistake?
Forgetting the "Hreflang" tags or pointing them to dead pages. This causes Google to get confused, often results in "Duplicate Content" penalties, and ensures the wrong version of your site shows up in the wrong country. (Fix this with a [LINK: technical-seo-audit-2025]).

### Do I need to host my site in the target country?
In the age of [LINK: serverless-architecture-2025] and high-speed CDNs (Content Delivery Networks), the physical server location is less important than it used to be. However, local IP addresses can still provide a small signal to regional search engines.

### Should I use my main brand name in every language?
Generally, yes. Consistency is key for building your identity as a "Global Entity." However, if your name has a negative or confusing meaning in another language, a regional "Brand Adapt" might be necessary. Check our [LINK: about] for how we approach global branding.
