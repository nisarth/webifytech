## The Core Pillars of a 2025 SEO Audit

A comprehensive audit focuses on three main areas: Crawlability, Indexability, and User Experience (Performance).

### 1. Crawlability: Can Google Find You?
Crawlability refers to the search engine's ability to navigate through your site's pages.
- **Check for Broken Links**: Use tools to identify 404 errors that waste "crawl budget."
- **Review Robots.txt**: Ensure you aren't accidentally blocking critical assets or pages.
- **Sitemap Health**: Verify your XML sitemap is up-to-date and submitted in Google Search Console.

### 2. Indexability: Can Google Understand You?
Once found, your pages must be indexed correctly.
- **Canonical Tags**: Prevent duplicate content issues by telling Google which version of a page is the "master."
- **Schema Markup**: Implement structured data so search engines can display rich snippets (prices, ratings, FAQs) in the search results.
- **Meta Tags**: Confirm every page has a unique, keyword-optimized title and meta description.

### 3. Performance: The 2025 Standard
Performance is now a direct ranking factor via Core Web Vitals.
- **LCP (Largest Contentful Paint)**: Your main content should load in under 2.5 seconds.
- **CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift)**: Elements shouldn't jump around as the page loads.
- **Mobile-First Design**: With 70% of traffic coming from mobile, your site must be flawless on small screens.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a technical SEO audit?
A technical SEO audit is a comprehensive evaluation of a website's technical health. It identifies issues that prevent search engines from crawling, indexing, or ranking the site's content effectively.

### How often should I run a technical SEO audit?
For most growing businesses, a full audit should be conducted quarterly. If you are making frequent updates to your site or running a large e-commerce store, a monthly "mini-audit" is highly recommended.

### What are the most common technical SEO issues?
The most common issues we see in 2025 include slow site speed, improper use of canonical tags, mobile responsiveness failures, and "orphan pages" that aren't linked from anywhere else on the site.

### How do I fix crawl errors on my website?
Identify the errors using Google Search Console's "Indexing" report. Common fixes include setting up 301 redirects for broken links and ensuring your server isn't returning 5xx errors during high-traffic periods.

### Does technical SEO affect content rankings?
Yes, absolutely. Technical SEO is the "container" for your content. If the container is broken, search engines will hesitate to rank the content inside it, regardless of its quality.
