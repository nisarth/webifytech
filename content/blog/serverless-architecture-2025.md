## What is Serverless in 2025?
"Serverless" doesn't mean there are no servers. It means that you, as a brand, don't have to manage them.
- **On-Demand Execution**: Your code only runs when it's needed (e.g., when someone clicks a button or visits a page).
- **Infinite Scalability**: If 10 people visit your site, the provider spawns 10 "functions." If 10 million people visit, it spawns 10 million. You don't have to lift a finger.
- **Pay-as-you-Go**: You only pay for the execution time you actually use. This is a massive part of our [LINK: web-development-cost-breakdown-2025].

## Benefit 2: Security-by-Isolation
In 2025, traditional servers are massive attack surfaces. A single vulnerability in an operating system can compromise an entire site.
Serverless functions, however, run in "Isolated Sandboxes." Once a function finishes its task (usually in milliseconds), the sandbox is destroyed.
- **Reduced Attack Surface**: There is no persistent "server" for a hacker to sit on.
- **Automatic Patching**: The cloud provider (like AWS or Vercel) is responsible for patching the underlying infrastructure, ensuring you're always protected by the latest [LINK: ecommerce-security-checklist-2025] protocols.

## FAQ

### Is serverless more expensive?
For very large applications with constant, predictable high-volume traffic, traditional servers *can* be cheaper per request. However, for almost all other businesses, the savings on maintenance, ops teams, and "idle server time" make serverless significantly more cost-effective. (See [LINK: web-development-cost-breakdown-2025]).

### What about "Cold Starts"?
In the past, serverless functions had a slight delay if they hadn't been used in a while (a "cold start"). In 2025, this issue has been largely solved through "Edge Warming" and more efficient runtimes. We use Next.js (as mentioned in our [LINK: complete-guide-web-development-2025]) which is specifically optimized for these environments.

### Can I run a database on serverless?
Yes! In 2025, we use "Serverless Databases" (like PlanetScale, Neon, or Supabase) that offer the same auto-scaling and pay-as-you-go benefits as your compute functions.
