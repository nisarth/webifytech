## The Foundation of Scale
1. **Statelessness**: Ensure your API servers don't store user session data locally. Use Redis or a similar shared store.
2. **Caching at the Edge**: Use CDN-level caching to serve common requests before they ever hit your database.
3. **Database Optimization**: Implementing read-replicas and efficient indexing is non-negotiable for high-performance [LINK: services].

## REST vs. GraphQL in 2025
While REST remains the standard for simplicity, we often recommend GraphQL for complex frontend requirements because it prevents "over-fetching" and allows for a single, unified data entry point.

## FAQ

### What makes an API scalable?
The ability to handle an increasing number of requests by adding more hardware resources (Horizontal scaling) without requiring a change in the software architecture.

### How do I secure my API?
Use OAuth2, Rate Limiting, and ensure all inputs are validated to prevent injection attacks, as outlined in our [LINK: website-security-threats-2025] guide.

### Which language is best for APIs?
Node.js (TypeScript) and Go are the top choices in 2025 for their concurrency models and speed.
