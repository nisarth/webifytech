## The Core Difference: Keywords vs. Conversations

Traditional SEO focuses on short, fragmented keywords (e.g., "web design cost"). Voice search focuses on full sentences and questions (e.g., "How much does it cost to build a website in 2025?").

### Key Pillars of Voice Search Optimization

1. **Conversational Keywords**: Focus on long-tail queries that start with Who, What, Where, When, and How.
2. **Local Intent**: A massive portion of voice searches are for local businesses. Ensure your [LINK: local-seo-guide-2025] is fully current.
3. **Featured Snippets (Position Zero)**: Voice assistants usually read the answer from the featured snippet. Structuring your content to win this spot is critical.
4. **Site Speed and Performance**: Voice searches happen on the move. If your site doesn't load instantly, the assistant will move to the next result.

### Is voice search different from traditional SEO?
Yes. It prioritizes local results, conversational language, and "Position Zero" snippets over the traditional blue links on a results page.

### How do I rank for voice search?
Focus on answering specific questions concisely, optimizing your Google Business Profile, and ensuring your mobile site speed is under 2 seconds.

### Do people actually use voice search for business?
Yes. In 2025, voice is used heavily for finding local services, researching prices, and navigating to locations. It's an essential part of the [LINK: about] phase of modern discovery.

### Does my website need special code for voice search?
Not exactly "special" code, but it does need high-quality schema markup and a mobile-responsive design that passes all Core Web Vital tests.

### What is the most common voice search?
The most common voice searches are for "near me" businesses and direct "how-to" or "how much" questions.
